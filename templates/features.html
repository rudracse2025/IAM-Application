Domain Isolation & Multi-Tenancy Security

Complete data separation ensures organizations maintain strict boundaries

1

Complete Data Isolation by Domain

Every user, request, and resource is tagged with a domain identifier. Users from Domain A cannot see, access, or interact with any data from Domain B or any other domain, regardless of their role or permission level.

2

Role-Based Access ≠ Cross-Domain Access

Even if an IT Admin has the highest permissions in their domain, they cannot access users, requests, or provisioning data from another domain. Access control is enforced at both the role level and the domain level independently.

3

Request Privacy & Confidentiality

HR requests from one organization remain completely private to that domain. An HR manager from Company A cannot view, search for, or discover employee information from Company B, even if they have HR permissions.

4

Isolated Audit Trails & Logs

Activity logs and audit trails are partitioned by domain. Each organization sees only its own request history, approval decisions, provisioning actions, and compliance records. Cross-domain queries are blocked at the database level.

5

Database-Level Access Enforcement

Domain separation is enforced at the application and database layers. Every database query filters results by domain, and direct attempts to bypass this restriction are logged as security events. No data leakage between tenants is possible.
